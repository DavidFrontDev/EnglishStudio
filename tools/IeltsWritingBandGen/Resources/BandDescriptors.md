# IELTS Writing Band Descriptors — Calibration Anchors

These are the **public** band descriptors condensed to one paragraph per band per criterion,
explicitly framed for generating calibration samples (essays that score *exactly* a given band).

Apply them strictly. A "band 6" sample must show the WEAKNESSES of band 6, not just be a
slightly less polished band 7. Likewise, a band 5 sample must show clear, frequent errors —
not be a band 6 with a single typo.

---

## Band 9 — Expert user

- **Task Achievement / Response**: fully satisfies all requirements; fully extended, nuanced ideas.
- **Coherence & Cohesion**: cohesion is so natural it attracts no attention; skilful paragraphing.
- **Lexical Resource**: wide vocabulary used with very natural and sophisticated control; rare slips.
- **Grammar**: wide range of structures used with full flexibility and accuracy; rare slips.

**Markers**: precise word choice ("notwithstanding", "underscores", "engender"), complex syntax
woven naturally, varied sentence openings, no awkwardness anywhere.

---

## Band 8 — Very good user

- **Task Achievement / Response**: sufficiently addresses all parts; well-developed ideas with relevant support.
- **Coherence & Cohesion**: logical sequencing; cohesion managed well; appropriate paragraphing.
- **Lexical Resource**: wide range; skilful use of less-common items; occasional inaccuracy in collocation/word-choice.
- **Grammar**: wide range; majority of sentences error-free; only occasional minor errors.

**Markers**: confident use of complex sentences; a few collocational slips ("strong rain" vs "heavy rain");
near-error-free grammar; sometimes a less-precise word.

---

## Band 7 — Good user

- **Task Achievement / Response**: addresses all parts; clear position; some ideas may be underdeveloped or over-general.
- **Coherence & Cohesion**: logical organisation; range of cohesive devices, occasionally over/under used; clear central topic in paragraphs.
- **Lexical Resource**: sufficient flexibility; some less-common items with awareness of style/collocation; some errors in word-formation/spelling.
- **Grammar**: variety of complex structures; frequent error-free sentences; good control of grammar and punctuation but a few errors.

**Markers**: clear thesis; some sentences are complex but a few feel formulaic; small but visible
spelling/collocation mistakes ("an information", "make a research"); cohesive devices feel a bit
inserted rather than natural.

---

## Band 6 — Competent user

- **Task Achievement / Response**: addresses requirements but format may slip; relevant position but conclusion unclear/repetitive; main ideas relevant but inadequately developed.
- **Coherence & Cohesion**: information organised; overall progression clear; cohesive devices used but ineffectively/mechanically; may lack reference and substitution.
- **Lexical Resource**: adequate range for the task; uses less common words with some inaccuracy; some errors in spelling/word formation that do not impede communication.
- **Grammar**: mix of simple and complex; makes some errors that rarely reduce communication.

**Markers**: ideas are relevant but feel surface-level; "Firstly… Secondly… In conclusion" linkers;
occasional wrong articles ("the people of today"); some complex sentences are awkward but
comprehensible; mixed tenses in places.

---

## Band 5 — Modest user

- **Task Achievement / Response**: generally addresses task but format may be inappropriate; recounts detail mechanically with no clear overview; position present but development unclear; main ideas hard to identify; may be repetitive.
- **Coherence & Cohesion**: some organisation but lacks overall progression; cohesive devices used inaccurately or over/under used; may be repetitive; paragraphing may be inadequate or missing.
- **Lexical Resource**: limited range, but adequate for the task; noticeable errors in spelling and/or word formation that may cause some difficulty for the reader.
- **Grammar**: limited range; attempts complex sentences but they tend to be less accurate than simple ones; frequent errors and punctuation may be faulty; errors can cause some difficulty for the reader.

**Markers**: lots of basic linkers ("And", "But", "So", "Also"); repeated vocabulary
("good… very good… really good"); subject-verb agreement slips ("people is", "he go");
mis-used articles or omitted articles; clauses joined with commas instead of conjunctions;
ideas restated rather than developed.

---

## Authenticity guidance

- A band-5 sample written by a real candidate will usually be only **120–170 words** for Task 1
  and **200–260 words** for Task 2 — not far above the minimum, sometimes a little under.
  A band-9 sample comfortably exceeds the minimums (~180w T1, ~290w T2) without padding.
- Real low-band essays have **uneven errors** — clusters of mistakes followed by a clean line, not
  a uniform sprinkling. Imitate that.
- Keep paragraphs short for bands 5–6 (1–3 sentences). Bands 7–9 sustain longer paragraphs.
- Do NOT write "as a band-5 candidate, I think…" — write the essay AS the candidate, not about them.
